# Horizon image relevance audit

This audit reviews the current generated horizon images as product communication, not as standalone art.

Scoring:

* **A:** the image explains the horizon at a glance.
* **B:** the image is relevant, but the key product behavior needs sharper props, action, or AR.
* **C:** the image has mood, but the product behavior is too abstract or generic.
* **D:** the image points at the wrong product idea.

## Global finding

The current horizon covers are visually coherent, but several of them solve the image with atmosphere, silhouettes, or generic UI geometry. That is not enough for Chummer6.

Every horizon image should show the future product as a lived table-facing scene:

* a GM making a decision
* a player seeing a consequence
* a runner reading useful AR
* an organizer closing a loop
* a creator producing a reusable artifact
* a map, packet, receipt, dossier, or approval object that visibly explains the feature

Readable AR is allowed when it is scene-fit and useful. It should label what the character is seeing, not decorate the picture.

## ALICE

Current relevance: **B-**

What works:

* The clinic/test-bay subject says "build simulation under consequences."
* The comparison/precheck/receipt chips fit the promise.
* The body-risk overlays point in the right direction.

What is weak:

* It reads more like cyberware trauma analysis than build comparison.
* The "two builds" idea is not visible enough.
* The AR labels are too medical and not enough build-facing.

Better image:

Show a player or GM comparing two runner build ghosts beside a live test subject or sim rig. Keep the clinic grit, but make the split decision visible: two transparent build silhouettes, different gear loadouts, and receipts explaining the tradeoff.

Useful AR labels:

* `BUILD A`
* `BUILD B`
* `EDGE COST`
* `LEGAL`
* `WEAK LINK`
* `RECEIPT`

## BLACK LEDGER

Current relevance: **C+**

What works:

* It gestures toward a city map and information layers.
* The chip labels match the horizon.

What is weak:

* It feels like an abstract dashboard, not a world after a tick.
* Pins are not specific enough.
* The image does not show districts, jobs, heat, intel, or consequences clearly.
* The viewer cannot tell what changed.

Better image:

Use a bright post-world-tick city map, presented as a table-scale AR projection or smartglass view after a GM resolves a run. Show district polygons, pins, heat rings, route lines, and small event cards. The map itself should be the hero.

Required map beats:

* at least five district or location pins
* one completed-run marker
* one open-job marker
* one intel-pending marker
* at least two faction-pressure markers
* visible heat or control overlays
* a GM/operator hand or runner silhouette reading the map

Useful AR labels:

* `WORLD TICK`
* `HEAT +2`
* `OPEN JOB`
* `RUN RESOLVED`
* `INTEL PENDING`
* `GM SIGNOFF`
* `PLAYER SAFE`
* `FACTION MOVE`

## JACKPOINT

Current relevance: **B**

What works:

* Dossier handoff is readable.
* The scene feels like evidence, receipts, and briefing material.
* The human stakes are clearer than most covers.

What is weak:

* It is too dark and static.
* It does not show narration, recap, or artifact production.
* The AR is mostly empty geometry instead of useful verification.

Better image:

Show a fixer or handler sliding a finished dossier pack across a table while a recorder, image card, and briefing reel status sit beside it. Keep the human handoff, but add artifact proof: packet seal, recap clip thumbnail, evidence strip, and publication receipt.

Useful AR labels:

* `DOSSIER READY`
* `RECAP SAFE`
* `SPOILER LOCK`
* `RECEIPT OK`
* `PUBLISH HOLD`

## KARMA FORGE

Current relevance: **D+**

What works:

* It has bold color and a "lab" feeling.
* The chips name rollback, witness, and seal drift.

What is weak:

* It looks like an empty sci-fi corridor, not governed house-rule work.
* There is no GM, creator, reviewer, rule packet, approval object, diff, or table consequence.
* The visible scene undersells the strong KARMA FORGE concept.

Better image:

Show a creator or GM standing at a rule-forge review bench with floating diff strips, approval seals, rollback paths, and a player-visible impact preview. The subject is not a literal blacksmith; it is a governed rules workbench under review.

Useful AR labels:

* `RULE DIFF`
* `APPROVED`
* `ROLLBACK`
* `PLAYER IMPACT`
* `CONFLICT`
* `PACK v1.2`

## NEXUS-PAN

Show a runner or player rejoining a messy session from a second device while a GM/table ghost, roster state, and last trusted receipt reconnect around them. The image should show repair after disruption, not just phone syncing.

Useful AR labels:

* `REJOIN`
* `STATE MATCH`
* `LAST RECEIPT`
* `ROSTER OK`
* `DRIFT FIXED`

## RUNSITE

Current relevance: **B+**

What works:

* The location-planning wall reads clearly.
* The GM/planner action is visible.
* The orange route map is relevant.

What is weak:

* It is still a flat wall plan, not an explorable runsite.
* Hotspots, player-safe versus GM-only layers, and route preview are missing.
* The image should show a space, not only a map.

Better image:

Show a runner or GM standing inside a projected warehouse/clinic/dock runsite model, with door hotspots, floor-plan overlays, route arrows, and spoiler-locked GM markers. Keep the physical planning gesture but add a partial 3D spatial preview.

Useful AR labels:

* `ENTRY`
* `HOTSPOT`
* `GM ONLY`
* `SAFE ROUTE`
* `FLOOR 2`
* `SPOILER LOCK`

## RUNBOOK PRESS

Current relevance: **B-**

What works:

* It reads like a printer or map copy, not a governed long-form publishing lane.

Useful AR labels:

* `APPROVED`
* `VERSIONED`

* `EXPORT READY`

## COMMUNITY HUB

## Priority replacement order

1. **BLACK LEDGER** - replace first. The image should be a world-tick map with pins and AR callouts.
2. **KARMA FORGE** - replace second. The current image misses the governed house-rule promise.
3. **COMMUNITY HUB** - replace third. It needs open-run workflow, not abstract skyline.
4. **RUNBOOK PRESS** - sharpen artifact publishing semantics.
5. **ALICE** - add clearer two-build comparison.
6. **NEXUS-PAN** - add stronger trust-repair/session continuity context.
7. **JACKPOINT** - lift brightness and add artifact verification labels.
8. **RUNSITE** - already close; add explorable hotspots and permission layers.

## Generation gate

Before accepting a new horizon image, the reviewer must be able to answer:

* What horizon is this without reading the title?
* What user action or product moment is visible?
* What changed because Chummer exists?
* What can humans still approve, reject, or control?
* Do readable AR labels help the character in the scene?
* Is the image vivid and polished rather than abstract, dim, or generic?
