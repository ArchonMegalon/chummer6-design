# Chummer next 90-day product advance

## Framing

The flagship closeout frontier is the last forced concentration pass that turns Chummer into a believable replacement product.

The next quarter should not reopen architecture cleanup, repo splitting, or horizon sprawl.
It should take the now-hard flagship bar and make it boring:

1. repeatable desktop release truth,
2. oracle-backed veteran replacement proof,
3. desktop-native trust and recovery,
4. conflict-safe continuity across claimed devices,
5. measured product-governor cadence.

The machine-readable control plane for this follow-on wave lives in `NEXT_90_DAY_PRODUCT_ADVANCE_REGISTRY.yaml`.

## Activation rule

This wave is seeded now so Fleet and repo owners can stage it immediately.

It activates when:

1. the current full-product flagship closeout frontier reaches zero open milestones,
2. `FLAGSHIP_PRODUCT_READINESS.generated.json` is green on `desktop_client` and the other flagship lanes,
3. the promoted desktop route is honest on Windows, macOS, and Linux.

Until then, this wave should shape follow-on work without preempting the current flagship closeout. Spare Fleet shards may already execute successor-wave slices, but they must not steal shards that are still assigned to the current closeout frontier.

## Ordering rule

1. Productionize native-host desktop release and promotion first.
2. Turn Chummer5a parity into oracle-backed release truth second.
3. Make desktop-native trust, recovery, and workspace continuity boring third.
4. Keep the product-governor loop measured and weekly instead of heroic and ad hoc.

## Wave 6 - make desktop promotion repeatable

### 101. Native-host desktop release train and promotion discipline
Owners: `chummer6-ui`, `chummer6-hub-registry`, `fleet`, `chummer6-design`
Exit: Windows, macOS, and Linux candidate-to-proof-to-promotion runs are one boring release lane with startup smoke, familiarity proof, installer truth, and rollback evidence.

### 102. Desktop-native claim, update, rollback, and support followthrough
Owners: `chummer6-hub`, `chummer6-hub-registry`, `chummer6-ui`, `fleet`
Exit: claim, update, rollback, recovery, and support followthrough happen from the installer or app, and all support/update surfaces agree on installed-build truth.

## Wave 7 - prove veteran replacement and mechanical trust

### 103. Chummer5a parity lab and veteran migration certification
Owners: `chummer6-design`, `chummer6-ui`, `executive-assistant`, `fleet`
Exit: screenshot compare packs, first-minute veteran tasks, import/export fixtures, and workflow maps exist for the serious Chummer5a replacement journeys.

### 104. Engine proof pack, explain budgets, and import-oracle discipline
Owners: `chummer6-core`, `chummer6-ui`, `chummer6-design`
Exit: every promoted desktop release carries engine proof, explain receipts, import-oracle status, and performance budgets that keep mechanical trust ahead of shell polish.

## Wave 8 - make continuity and product-governor boring

### 105. Workspace restore, entitlement sync, and conflict-safe continuity
Owners: `chummer6-hub`, `chummer6-ui`, `chummer6-mobile`, `chummer6-core`
Exit: claimed users can restore workspace, entitlement, last context, and safe continuation across desktop and mobile with explicit stale and conflict posture.

### 106. Product-governor weekly adoption and measured rollout loop
Owners: `fleet`, `executive-assistant`, `chummer6-design`, `chummer6-hub`
Exit: launch, freeze, canary, rollback, feedback followthrough, and public status copy all compile from the same measured readiness and parity truth.

## Non-goals for this quarter

This wave is not the time to:

* reopen repo-split cleanup,
* widen media-first experimentation,
* expand horizons into product-critical scope,
* trade veteran trust for novelty,
* let green milestone coverage masquerade as lived product truth.
